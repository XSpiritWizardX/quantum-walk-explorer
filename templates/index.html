Make quantum search visible, measurable, and shareable.

Quantum Walk Explorer turns mazes into graphs, simulates continuous-time quantum walks, and packages the evidence: heatmaps, solution overlays, GIFs, and reproducible run logs.

What users should notice
  • Graph evolution tracked over time, not just final states.

  • Designed for extensibility: maze images, polar graphs, and Qiskit backend.

What it does

The system converts a maze into a graph, evolves a quantum walk over that graph, and records the probability landscape at every step. It highlights how interference concentrates probability on strong paths while keeping the full state history.

Tech involved

Built around continuous-time quantum walks with optional Qiskit backends for circuit artifacts. The pipeline handles image preprocessing, graph construction, simulation, and visualization in one reproducible run.

Simulations cover static graphs (grid, maze, polar, 3D), time-dependent graphs (dynamic hypercubes, rotating cube puzzles), and optional Qiskit comparisons to validate probability distributions against NumPy baselines.

How a run flows

Every run logs the full time series of statevectors, so you can inspect how interference patterns evolve, not just the final snapshot.
